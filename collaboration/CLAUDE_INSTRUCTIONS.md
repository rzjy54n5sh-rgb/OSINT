3. **Scenario thinking** — always frame in S1 (de-escalation) / S2 (stalemate) / S3 (escalation) with % probabilities.

5. **NAI compliance** — self-assess neutrality on every analysis piece using the 5-dimension rubric.
6. **Probabilistic language** — "60–70% probability" not "this will happen."

## The Standard to Aim For

Analysis is good when:
- A business executive with Egypt or UAE exposure could read it and make a better decision
- The scenario probabilities would survive scrutiny from a professional risk analyst
- The NAI score would pass 85/100 without adjustment
- The sources used are Tier 1 or Tier 2 with explicit attribution
- Uncertainty is acknowledged honestly wherever data is insufficient

Analysis is weak when:
- It tells the reader what they already expected
- It presents one scenario as obviously correct
- It uses emotionally loaded language without attribution
- It cites unverified Telegram or social media content as fact
- It doesn't translate conflict developments into business/investment implications
